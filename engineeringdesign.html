Transmission filters for the automotive industry

Design Engineer Intern

Filtran / June - September 2016

​Filtran, a part of Filtration Group, is a leading global supplier of automatic transmission filtration solutions to the automotive industry and aftermarket. Filtran is ISO-9000, ISO-14001, TS-16949 certified.
New Pressure Filter / Allison Transmission

After Allison provided a significantly-revised environment, we needed to design a new check valve and bypass valve, and send preliminary drafts to our supplier.
Suction Filter Modification/ Allison Transmission

To parallel environmental changes from the client, the ribbed shelf needed an extension for additional structural stability.
Vibration Testing Fixture / Filtran

The lab's current vibration test fixture had custom mounting holes drilled in for each filter ever tested. Over time, these holes and labels have begun to seriously overlap and increase setup times. I created a new fixture (right) with an alphanumeric grid so that the design engineers can instead simply call out mounting holes for each filter and its respective adapter fixtures.
Check Valve / American Axle & Manufacturing

Based on an existing plastic check valve, I designed a new metal version based on hardness and threading strength requirements. The proposed design includes a cap and spring that forces down the ball (pictured, right).
Self-Positioning Screen Filter / General Motors

This more unusual environment provided little space for a traditional filter that uses fasteners. I designed one with a rubber ring and angled walls that self-positions on assembly.
Reinforced Jet Nozzle / Ford

Manufactur­ing Engineering

Bionic Wrench design & assembly

Manufacturing Engineering Capstone Project

Northwestern University / March - June 2017

​The Bionic Wrench™, created by Loggerhead Tools, offered a prime opportunity to analyze and rehearse modern manufacturing methods. My team analyzed its design and assembled the wrench under a mass-manufacturing situation, using rivet fixtures, floor planning, and equipment best for replicating the quality of the OEM.
Analysis and Experimentation

The analysis of the wrench included disassembly, using an optical comparator, reconstructing it in CAD, and prototyping fixtures.

Motorized Breadfruit Peeler

Uplifting farmers and food processors in Jamaica and Haiti

Motorized Breadfruit Peeler Capstone Project

Northwestern University / January - July 2016

​As a major crop in Jamaica, breadfruit can po­ten­tial­ly strengthen food security and en­tre­pre­neurial ventures on the island. ​The Trees That Feed Foundation wanted to improve the throughput of breadfruit processing, in particular peel removal. The final prototype "Bolt" peels the breadfruit's sticky, rough skin in as little as 10 seconds compared to hand-peeling. Also considered was manu­fac­tur­ing and supply costs, and manual operation.
We also got featured on Northwestern Ideas.
Initial Brainstorming

Using input from peers, contacts in Jamaica, and existing products in similar markets, my gathered an initial pool of ideas. At this stage, it was important to keep the problem definition broad and be open to more unusual ideas, less we accidentally rule out one that turns out to be the best solution. These sketches represent just a fraction of the ideas.
Iteration 1 - 2 (January - March)

To turn breadfruit into flour, it must go through peeling, shredding, drying, and grinding. Based on our client's documentation and some of our end-user contacts, a pedal- or hand-powered solution seemed to be the right path.
Jamaica Field Studies (March)

In Jamaica, we found that initial assumptions and previous documentation was wrong or outdated. However, thanks to the farmers, students, and food processing workers we met with, the project objective and requirements became more clear. Among our new findings was a reliable electric grid, and a distaste for "backward" manual-powered machinery.
Iteration 3 (April)

Based on notes from the Jamaica trip, the mockups took on a more mechanized form.
Iteration 4 (April-June)

​Redesign of blade arm and self-retraction mechanism.

Segal Design Institute Projects

Northwestern University / June - September 2015

​The Segal interns work on several mechanical and biomedical projects involving user research, design refinement, and prototype fabrication. Clients ranged from a child with motor impairment at the Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago, to doctors at the University of Ibadan, Nigeria, to many other local organizations. ​While I was involved in all projects, I worked significantly on several in particular.
Floor Outlet Covers (Segal)

​​The outlet covers at the Segal building were worn out and had become tripping hazards. As co-lead, I determined our design goal, and we managed the design iteration process, the mock-up and prototype material sourcing, and schedule benchmarks.
Infant Surgery Warming Pad (University of Ibadan)

Nigerian infants who require gastrointestinal surgery are unable to get it in a timely fashion due to financial and infrastructural complications. Doctors at the University of Ibadan are looking into alternatives of expensive high-end medical devices. The Infant Warming Pad is one solution that is affordable and mechanically simple. I worked on the machining and assembly of the physical electronics and external structure, including the circuit boards, wiring, and power supply housing.
North Garage Car Team Space (NU marketing team)

​The newly-constructed North Garage on the Northwestern Campus included a space devoted to the three student car teams in order to ease overcrowding in the other car teams spaces. Northwestern University's engineering school marketing team wanted to place decals in the space. I created floor and wall drafts and a CAD model to facilitate their planning.
