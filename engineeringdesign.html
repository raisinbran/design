Vibration Testing Fixture / Filtran

The lab's current vibration test fixture had custom mounting holes drilled in for each filter ever tested. Over time, these holes and labels have begun to seriously overlap and increase setup times. I created a new fixture (right) with an alphanumeric grid so that the design engineers can instead simply call out mounting holes for each filter and its respective adapter fixtures.
Check Valve / American Axle & Manufacturing

Based on an existing plastic check valve, I designed a new metal version based on hardness and threading strength requirements. The proposed design includes a cap and spring that forces down the ball (pictured, right).
Self-Positioning Screen Filter / General Motors

This more unusual environment provided little space for a traditional filter that uses fasteners. I designed one with a rubber ring and angled walls that self-positions on assembly.

Bionic Wrench Design Study

Manufacturing Engineering Capstone Project

Northwestern University / March - June 2017

​The Bionic Wrench™, created by Loggerhead Tools, offered a prime opportunity to analyze and rehearse modern manufacturing methods. My team analyzed its design and assembled the wrench under a mass-manufacturing situation, using rivet fixtures, floor planning, and equipment best for replicating the quality of the OEM.

Motorized Breadfruit Peeler

Uplifting farmers and food processors in Jamaica and Haiti

Motorized Breadfruit Peeler Capstone Project

Northwestern University & Jamaica / January - July 2016

​As a major crop in Jamaica, breadfruit can po­ten­tial­ly strengthen food security and en­tre­pre­neurial ventures on the island. ​The Trees That Feed Foundation wanted to improve the throughput of breadfruit processing, in particular peel removal. The final prototype "Bolt" peels the breadfruit's sticky, rough skin in as little as 10 seconds compared to hand-peeling. Also considered was manu­fac­tur­ing and supply costs, and manual operation.
We also got featured on Northwestern Ideas.
Initial Brainstorming

Using input from peers, contacts in Jamaica, and existing products in similar markets, my gathered an initial pool of ideas. At this stage, it was important to keep the problem definition broad and be open to more unusual ideas, less we accidentally rule out one that turns out to be the best solution. These sketches represent just a fraction of the ideas.
Iteration 1 - 2 (January - March)

To turn breadfruit into flour, it must go through peeling, shredding, drying, and grinding, in that order. Based on our client's documentation and some of our end-user contacts, a pedal- or hand-powered solution seemed to be the right path.
Jamaica Field Studies (March)

In Jamaica, we found that initial assumptions and previous documentation was wrong or outdated. However, thanks to the farmers, students, and food processing workers we met with, the project objective and requirements became more clear. Among our new findings was a reliable electric grid and a distaste for "backwards" manual-powered machinery.
Iteration 3 (April)

Based on notes from the Jamaica trip, the mockups took on a more mechanized form.

Material Design

Replace­ment surgical femur study

Material Selection Design

March 2016

​Due to a small market, femur replacements have seen little development. Current types, cobalt-chromium-molybdenum and titanium-aluminum, are both expensive and difficult to machine. Using existing literature and CES EduPack, we narrowed down the choices to titanium-aluminum-vanadium, expensive but extremely strong, and titanium grade 4, which is cheaper but still relatively stronger than current femur materials on the market. Our objective was minimum cost and density, and our constraint was femur geometry.

"Candy Som­brero" Toy

Computer-aided manufacturing & injection molding

"Candy Sombrero" Toy Figure

January - March 2016

​As part of a program to promote engineering, a group of fourth graders tasked us with toys to injection-mold using polypropylene. Keeping in mind the limits of color mixing and part size on the molds, my team separated our toy, a "candy sombrero" figure into three parts: the sombrero, the main body, and the mustache.
Initial Drawing

With a guiding hand from engineering students, the fourth ​​graders draw toy designs that can be injection molded. We're responsible for transforming one of the drawings into a physical product capable of mass production.
Mold Design and First Run

In our first run, we found the polypropelene unsurprisingly sensitive to small changes. The sombrero hat shows a noticeable flow mark (right) where we overdid the runner diameter. An error in the body mold offset the tabs that connected the two halves together, requiring an entirely new mold set to be made.
Mold Redesign and Second (Final) Run

​Not eager to invest more time shepherding the CNC mills, we cautiously redesigned and machined a second pair of body molds. We also simplified the body into one part, with no halves; in exchange, two body parts could now fit on the mold to reduce injection time. This version became the final prototype.
Computer-Aided Manufacturing Setup

​Design and setup of the molds in Siemens NX.

Instruction simulation on Siemens NX: end mill toolbit, surface finishing run.

"Infra­Shield" Ballistic Wall

Ballistic-resistant protection for electrical transformers

"InfraShield" Ballistic-Resistant Wall

March - June 2014

​After the April 2013 Silicon Valley substation incident, our client showed interest in improving protection of America's electric grid. In terms of small-arms fire, we identified the cylindrical bushings as one weak point that could easily shut down a substation transformer. The InfraShield both obstructs line of sight from the substation perimeter fence and absorbs ballistic impacts, while its conservative design reduces construction cost.
Background

Courtesy WSJ .

Bushings, tall cylinders (left) commonly insulated in ceramic, allow electricity to travel safely from the transformer to the overhead wires and out of the substation. ​​By nature of their material, they are brittle and cannot absorb large focused points of energy, such as a bullet. An infographic (right) of the Silicon Valley substation incident demonstrates the vulnerability of the electric grid to determined attackers.
Research

Along with professors and experts in electrical engineering and material science, we met with ComEd, the electric utility for Chicago and much of Illinois.
