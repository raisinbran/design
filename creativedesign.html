Bushings, tall cylinders (left) commonly insulated in ceramic, allow electricity to travel safely from the transformer to the overhead wires and out of the substation. ​​By nature of their material, they are brittle and cannot absorb large focused points of energy, such as a bullet. An infographic (right) of the Silicon Valley substation incident demonstrates the vulnerability of the electric grid to determined attackers.
